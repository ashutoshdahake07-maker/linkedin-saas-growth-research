When we are doing positioning work, we need to be customer-obsessed but competitor-aware (and never the other way around). 

In B2B, your product is not chosen in a vacuum. Customers will compare you to what they are doing today (at a minimum) and also to a short list of direct competitors. We need to understand how customers evaluate alternatives and why they choose us. Understanding competitors alone doesn't give us what we need to win deals. The key to winning is customer insight within a competitive context. 

I was reminded today of the famous Avis positioning of "We try harder," which focused on differentiating their customer service from Hertz (the market leader at the time). Marketing folk love that story but in the end, they both lost the market. Enterprise understood that service was important, but renters also cared about better car choices, and convenient locations. Today Enterprise is twice the size of Hertz and Avis combined. 

Understand your competition, but obsess over customer value.
