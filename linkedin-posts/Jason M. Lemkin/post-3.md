Do you treat your 20 year customers ... worse than your new ones?

You probably do, as crazy as that sounds. We've talked enough about our really, really rough experience with Adobe Marketo. They broke our unsubcribe link for weeks, blamed us, then blamed Beehiv (which we don't use), then blamed Salesforce, then forced us to get on multiple calls with no resolution.

About as bad an experience as you can get. So bad, we had to just vibe code our own Unsubscribe experience in Replit.

But honestly? It's worse that that. Because I literally was one of the First 10 customers ever of Marketo -- in 2006! The founders sold me personally in the office.

Now, we've used them through 3 legal entities and took a break for a few years when Mailchimp was all we used.

Still, all-in-all, we've been a 20 year customer.

And what did we get? Years of price increases, and zero help when we needed it. 

It sounds critical, and it is, but it points out one large issue in B2B: we bend over backwards to get new customers, onboard them, FDE them, etc. And then mostly, we abandon them. And just raise prices on them.

The worst case study is Marketo. A 20 year customer, one of their first 10 customers. And all we got ... was blamed.
