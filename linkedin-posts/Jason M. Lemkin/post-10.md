
When growth slows ... is it sales + marketing's fault, or the product's fault?

This has led to office fights, drama, accusations, finger pointing, and more since the dawn of B2B software.

But the answer is different today. Why?

Because until the past 18 months or so, until the rise of agentic products, B2B software mostly was the same for years on end.

You'd add features, fix debt, catch up to competitors, and every few years, add a new product.

But it was mostly static. And so sales and marketing's goal was to jam more and more folks into the funnel, and get more and more of them to buy.

Today, products are incredibly fluid, and get stale incredibly quickly. You can't go 3-5 years between major releases.

Claude itself is literally 100x better than 12 months ago.

And so, while some of the "fault" may still be in sales and marketing, more likely it's a product problem today.

The pace of change, improvement, competition and more is like nothing we've seen before. Fire your VP of Sales if you want. But if your product isn't as competitive as it was 6-18 months ago, I doubt it will help.
