At 21 I got diagnosed with high-functioning anxiety.
At 22 I got diagnosed with depression and PTSD. 
By 23 I had a collection of labels I hid behind. 

Anxiety became my identity. Depression was a habit. 

By 24, I noticed a life-changing pattern:

I wasn't working on myself not because I was depressed. I was depressed because I wasn't working on myself. 

I wasn't building a career because I had anxiety.
I had anxiety because I wasn't building a career. 

At any time, you can choose to simply stop this. 

You can start doing things the labels said you couldn't.
You can act like the person the labels said you couldn't.

I did this. In 6 months I proved every label wrong. 

To anyone who's struggling with their mental health:

Don't let the label define who you are.

Let it guide you in the right direction. Understand yourself and how your brain works. 

But please, do not let it stop you from becoming the person you know you can become. 

Love, someone who's not had a panic attack in 3+ years.
