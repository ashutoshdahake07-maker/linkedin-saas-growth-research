every post i write goes through a 9-point checklist.

before i hit publish, i ask myself (now with the help if AI) these questions:

1. could anyone else have written this?
if yes, i rewrite it. if it sounds generic, it won't land.

2. does the hook stop the scroll in 2 lines?
you have 2 seconds. maybe less. the hook has to earn the click.

3. is there at least one specific number, name, or detail?
specifics build trust. vague posts get scrolled past.

4. does it sound like me talking, not writing?
if it reads like a blog post, it's wrong. linkedin is a conversation.

5. does my ICP feel their own gap when reading this?
the best posts hold up a mirror. people should see themselves.

6. is the offer woven in naturally?
if it feels like a pitch, i've lost. the value should lead, not the ask.

7. does the CTA invite real participation?
"what do you think?" is lazy. i want specific, thoughtful responses.

8. would i actually post this?
gut check. if something feels off, it probably is.

9. does this move someone closer to human, relationship-driven selling?
this is the north star. every post should reinforce that AI alone isn't the answer — humans are.

why do i do this?

because most content on linkedin is forgettable.

it's either AI slop or generic advice that could come from anyone.

i've built two $100M+ companies. i've written a WSJ bestseller. i've helped 100+ fractional GTM leaders build their businesses.

if i'm going to post, it better be worth reading.

this checklist keeps me honest.
