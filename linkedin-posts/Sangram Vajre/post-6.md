most B2B founders don't have a marketing strategy problem.
they have an operating system problem.

Dave Gerhardt said it last week (somewhere) and i haven't been able to stop thinking about it — because it's exactly what we see when we audit companies.

the strategy is usually fine. the frameworks exist. the ideas are there. 

but nothing connects.

demand gen runs one play. sales runs another. customer success is doing its own thing. 

and oh! and the CEO is sitting in the middle wondering why growth feels so random.

so they hire a new CMO. or bring in an agency. or switch to a new channel. and six months later, they're having the same conversation.

here's what i've learned after having 2000 B2B companies gone thru our GTM assessment: 

you can't fix a broken operating system with a new strategy. 

you'll just run the new strategy on the same broken foundation and get the same inconsistent results.

the GTM OS exists for exactly this reason. eight pillars. all connected. 

positioning feeds ICP. ICP feeds demand gen. demand gen feeds pipeline. pipeline feeds revenue. 

and when one breaks, everything downstream breaks with it.

the question for every founder isn't "what's our next marketing strategy?"

it's "do we actually have a GTM operating system — or just a collection of tactics that nobody connects?"

those are very different problems. and they need very different solutions.

isn't it?
