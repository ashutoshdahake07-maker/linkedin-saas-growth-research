Security teams are becoming engineering teams.

 I sat down with Jonathan Jaffe, CISO at Lemonade, for Office Hours to discuss what changes when both attackers & defenders are automated. A few ideas stuck with me.

 AI is just as powerful for defenders as it is for attackers. The fear narrative misses that every vendor in your stack is racing to ship the same capabilities. Defenders harden everywhere, simultaneously.

 The window of exploitability is narrowing. Yes, AI writes more vulnerable code. But that same code gets reviewed, pen-tested, & patched faster than any human pipeline. The total number of bugs in any piece of software is finite.

 As resolution velocity rises, software becomes more resilient.

 At Lemonade, every security person is an engineer. They built their own AI platform with agents on top : one reads threat intel, another checks whether a vulnerable method is actually called in production code. As Jonathan put
 it, "automation is the only way to deal with the scale of what's coming at us now."

 Every agent needs an identity. On a single endpoint, we could be running 200 or 10,000 agents. Each needs to be numbered & governed by policy at the point of action. Current IAM systems were not designed for this.

 The mission of the security profession is shifting : from managing people to architecting the automated policies that govern an agentic world. It's a bright future for security & for the people who build it.
