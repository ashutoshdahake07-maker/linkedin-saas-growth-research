I know there are a ton of "gurus" out there sharing their "personalization hack secrets" for your outbound campaigns but I've come to believe that they are all full of shit.

Just look at the state of cold email outbound today based on hyper-AI-personalization.

There is a whole Y Combinator thread of Founder/CEOs sharing these with each other making fun of how horrible outbound sales personalization has become (image below).

It started gently and innocently.

Now it’s gotten out of control—mentioning a prospect’s city or a half-baked reference to a LinkedIn post, all in an attempt to sound clever or human.

Guess what? Trying to be clever is about you. It’s self-centered and doesn’t add value.

If I wanted to chit-chat about the weather, I would talk to my mother.

If you’re showing up in my inbox for business reasons, tell me,

Why should I care? Why should I listen? Why should I trust you?

And then get out of my inbox.

With AI, all of this is about to get even worse.

People are going to run AI agents to say something smart about someone’s last five tweets or someone’s college, and it’s going to be a shit show.

My stance: DON’T BOTHER.

Use AI for more productive cases. Instead: → Be truthful, direct, and succinct → Give your prospect a reason to believe you and a clear next step → Explain specifically and concretely what you do, why they should care, and why you’re credible

If you can do that in a few sentences, at least I see you’re somebody who respects my time and can be trusted. It’ll be refreshing.

Because all the other stuff is just noise. You’ll stand out amongst the bullshit.
