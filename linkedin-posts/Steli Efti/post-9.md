 I’m going to say something you may not like: sales is not hard.

It’s people who are difficult.

I’ve been preaching this for years, and it’s never been truer than it is today.

What makes sales feel hard isn’t the actual work. It’s not writing an email, picking up the phone, or asking for the close.

It’s YOU.

Your doubts. Your hesitations. Your fear of rejection. Your ego.

That’s what slows you down and leads to poor choices.

Sales itself is not rocket science. There are jobs out there that are actually HARD. Sales is challenging, but it’s simple.

The problem is discomfort. Every meaningful action in sales requires you to step outside your comfort zone. Following up one more time. TEN more times! Asking the blunt question. Calling when you’d rather procrastinate.

And that’s why my biggest advice to you is this: Pain and discomfort are the signal.

They’re the signal that you’re going in the right direction.
