I've talked to dozens of founders and CEOs in the past 2 weeks about AI Agents.

And two things are consistent across all these conversations: 

Security and Observability

We all want to deploy agents live in our businesses, but very few of us trust that they're going to be secure, and that they're going to do what we expect of them.

I've long (ok long in AI time is skewed) known that observability for AI agents is going to be important...we need the equivalent of server logs for them. Things we can query, build reports on, etc. I know the big infra players are working on this. 

But security is really the first thing we have to crack. Even if they're great (yeah I'm looking at you my lobster friends) but not secure, then it'll never go mainstream. 

AgentZero is actually a great example of this. It's already being deployed in the enterprise, because it's very much "the linux of AI agents" - it's bare metal, comes with no permissions or access, and you have to opt it in to everything. 

We'll probably land somewhere in the middle, but the agent platform that gets widely adopted into companies will have extremely robust Security and Observability.
