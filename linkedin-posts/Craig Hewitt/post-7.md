
Your CRM is a liability, not an asset. 

Let me explain:

If your CRM is anything like mine it’s a graveyard of data. 

Stale records cause you didn’t update that one field 3 months ago. 

Lost context because your crm didn’t get the LinkedIn DM a lead sent you. 

Missing process compliance cause we’re all human and just forget to do stuff. (Yup I didn’t send that follow up to the lead 6 weeks ago).

So, what is supposed to the source of truth in most businesses is instead incomplete, inaccurate, and inflexible. 

It’s a liability, an impediment to your sales teams moving faster and closing more deals. 

Same exact analogy as many of us are seeing with website builders these days. 

It’s way easier to just talk to Claude Code, programmatically have it bring in new info from dedicated channels, and be the nimble tool we all want it to be in the AI era. 

But my CRM (and I’d guess yours too) is stuck in 2012

- custom fields for everything 
- automations that break
- stale/bad/innacurate data

We need to think of Sales & GTM systems just like we do Claude Code or Codex. 

It should be an agent. 

It should have all of the context, in a structured but flexible manner. 

It should have a CLI

It should natively integrate with your call recorder, social media, email, operations software. I mean like it should plug directly into it. Not Zapier-esque rigid workflows. 

If you take the agentic mindset to its natural next step it removes all of the contraints of conventional SaaS and just delivers straight value and productivity. 

Not adding more work, worse data, and less output. 

This is a $1B kinda problem.
