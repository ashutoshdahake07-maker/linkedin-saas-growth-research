
The layoffs keep coming.

➡ Today: Webflow saying they're "Evolving Webflow for the agentic web."
➡ Yesterday: Wix laid off 20% of their team
➡ Last week: Clickup laid off 22% of their team.

Companies are under massive pressure from their boards and investors to "Do AI" - but most have no idea what that means.

The underlying theme in all these companies I talk with: 

"We're going to do more, with less"

More output, more features, more deliverables, with less people. 

It's an OK model, but I think that unless you're at the same time investing in training, technology, and process for the team left behind you're going to be left with a skeleton of a company. 

Truth is, i see very few companies implementing this strategy shift well.
