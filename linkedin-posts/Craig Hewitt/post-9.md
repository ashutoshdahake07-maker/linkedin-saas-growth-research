The hardest part of AI adoption isn't the technology. It's getting your whole team to actually use it.

At Castos, every department is building with AI. 

Not because I mandated it, because we built a culture around it.

Here's what I hear in Slack every week:

- "I was looking at this API and built us a tool that [insanely useful thing]"
- "I was playing around in Claude Code and put this together"
- "I automated [manual process], here's the workflow"

Dev. Support. Product. Marketing. Ops.

Everyone. Not just engineering.

This didn't happen by accident. It took intentional effort over months:

- Making AI tools available to every team member, not just devs
- Celebrating when non-technical people built something with AI
- Having honest (sometimes hard) conversations about what's changing

Most companies buy AI tools and wonder why nobody uses them. The tool isn't the bottleneck. The culture is.

We're at the point now where the whole team is excited about what's coming next.

If you're a founder and AI adoption has stalled at your company, the fix probably isn't better tools...it's better leadership around the tools you already have
