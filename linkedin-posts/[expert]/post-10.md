What if AI kills the placebo effect in sales?

AI is changing sales—predicting outcomes, scoring leads, deciding who’s “worth” our time. 

The danger comes when we stop believing success is possible just because the data says so. 

Sales isn’t just about data, strategy, and logic. It requires a huge amount of faith, something that can’t be quantified.

Humans need the courage to chase a deal that “shouldn’t” close. The vision to see an opportunity where others don’t. 

The belief that you can turn things around, even when the odds are stacked against you. 

Sales is the ultimate placebo effect—it only works if you believe it CAN work.

I remember a story about a RevOps guy who replaced half of the negative customer notes in a CRM (“This prospect is rude," "They hate our product," "Never call again”) with fake positive ones. 

He split the leads among his team and the sales team with the "positive" notes closed 5–10x more deals. 

Why? Because they believed they had a shot. They showed up differently: more confident, more persistent, more open. 

But what is AI doing to sales today? Drowning us in data that predicts our outcomes for us:

🚫 This lead is unlikely to convert. 
🚫 This prospect probably won’t open your email. 
🚫 This account is a lost cause. 

If we trust the data blindly, we stop trying. We stop believing. And that’s when we actually lose.

You can’t outsource faith and conviction to an AI SDR. 

Here’s how I look at it: 𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮 𝗶𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗽𝗮𝘀𝘁. 𝗕𝗲𝗹𝗶𝗲𝗳 𝗰𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗲𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗳𝘂𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲.

AI can make sales smarter, but if we’re not careful, it’ll strip away the one thing that makes us great—our ability to believe in possibilities others can’t see yet. 

So, what do you think? Are we using AI to get better, or are we letting it rob us of our faith?
