More info = more sales is a convenient lie. 

There’s a growing belief in sales and marketing that if a buyer just had enough information, they’d close themselves.

This is bullshit.

That’s not how buying decisions work. That’s how people want to believe they work—logically, clean, step-by-step. But it’s delusional!

People don’t buy based on logic. They buy based on EMOTION.

And when the stakes are high, buyers want certainty, trust, and leadership.

We’re living in a time where:

- Attention spans are collapsing
- Expertise is collapsing
- Certainty is not increasing with more data → it’s decreasing
- You can throw all of the possible information at a buyer, and they’ll still want to get on a call.

Why?

Because people don’t just want information.

They want energy. They want clarity. They want certainty. They want to talk to a human who can guide them.

They want someone who can say:

“Here’s what’s right for you. Here’s what’s not. I’ve seen this before. Let me help you make the right call.”

That’s not just selling. That’s leadership.

The future of sales belongs to people who can lead—not just pitch.

The reps who can show up with real expertise, real conviction, and say: “I’ve got you. Let’s figure this out together.” 

That’s the skill that’s becoming rare! 

If you’re in sales today: Double down on the one thing that AI, product tours, and slide decks will never replace—your ability to earn trust and lead people forward.

That’s the future. And it’s human as hell
