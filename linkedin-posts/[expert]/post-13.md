If you want to keep your sales job in the age of AI, you need to master these 3 things asap: Clarity, vision, and leadership

These aren't attributes you are born with, they are habits that have to be practiced daily.

I recently reviewed a bunch of tech sales calls, and here’s what’s driving me crazy.

Reps are getting lost in the weeds. They’re reacting instead of directing. 

Calls are a back-and-forth game of feature questions and scattered conversations. 

Guess who's going to be better and cheaper than you in answering lots of random feature and technology questions? Correct! AI!

So what is left for you to do? After creating deep understanding and connection with a prospect, your job is to 𝗹𝗲𝗮𝗱. 

Here’s how to stand out:

⭐ Start with the big picture. Before diving into features and price, ask: Why are we having this conversation? What are your actual goals?

⭐ Reframe the discussion around value. If they’re worried about cost, don’t negotiate—help them understand the ROI of solving their real problem.

⭐ Take control. Prospects have a million questions, but not all of them matter. Help them prioritize what will actually move the needle.

Next time you hop on a call, take 5 minutes beforehand and map out:

- The purpose of the call and ideal action to be taken
 -The key decision or action that needs to happen
 -The most important question you need to ask

Clarity is rare. Vision is rare. Be the one who brings it.
