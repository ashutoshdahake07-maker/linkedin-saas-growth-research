Speed-to-lead isn’t just a math problem. It’s a pain avoidance problem. 

Everybody knows you should call a lead within 5 minutes.

Almost nobody actually does it.

And it’s not because people don’t know the data. Every founder and sales leader has heard the stats a thousand times.

The real problem is simpler: humans avoid painful things.

Calling a lead means risking rejection. Getting ignored. Hearing everything from “not interested” to “f*ck you.” So instead, founders and sellers do literally anything else first. 

They tell themselves they have to "prepare" for the call. They review notes they don’t really need to review. They tinker with Claude to generate the perfect phone script. They pour another cup of coffee. 

It takes a tremendous amount of force to overcome the pain and pick up the phone. This has always been true of sales and it will always be true as long as humans are humans. 

That’s what makes AI voice interesting to me. Not because it replaces human sales conversations. Because it removes the emotional friction that prevents those conversations from happening in the first place.

An AI voice agent doesn’t have to avoid pain. It doesn’t get emotionally drained. It doesn’t need to take a long walk after a bad call. 

You, as a human, don't even have to deal with the question of “should I call now or not?”

With AI, the call already happened, and it’s being transferred to you when the lead is actually ready to talk. 

Whenever someone says “AI will replace salespeople,” I just have to shake my head because I know they’ve got it all wrong. 

What if you just removed the human from the most painful first touch and pushed them further down the funnel, where they matter more?

This is the real unlock of Chloe, the AI voice sales teammate built into Close. Chloe can have the hardest conversations, absorb the pain, and free humans up to do better, more impactful work. 

We’re wrapping up our Chloe Beta and already seeing businesses using Chloe to collapse their speed-to-lead by 10x.

Very excited about the future of selling :)
