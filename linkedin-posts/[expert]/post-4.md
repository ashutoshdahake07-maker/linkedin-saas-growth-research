Post 4

The most surprising thing we learned in Chloe Beta (Voice AI Agent) had nothing to do with the technology.

It was about human behavior.

Here's what we expected: people would get a call and hear "I'm a voice AI" and hang up.

We expected skepticism and resistance, but what we heard instead was intense curiosity.

Chloe discloses upfront: "Just so you know, I'm a voice AI. This is a recorded line."

And you can hear the hesitation in the person’s pause. They’re working it out in their heads: “This is AI. Should I hang up?”

Then Chloe asks a question. Something direct and relevant.

And you can almost hear the person on the other end decide: “Ok, fine. I'll just answer this one and then I'll hang up.”

Then Chloe responds in a way that feels real. She asks them another question. They answer that one too.

After a minute on the phone with Chloe, the resistance just dissolves. The energy shifts. They start asking their own questions.

People stop thinking about whether they're talking to AI and start thinking about what they're actually trying to figure out.

By the end of the call, I've heard prospects say things like:

"This is incredible."
"Can I buy you too?"
"Are you a feature of Close? I want this for my business."

I was floored listening to people go from "should I hang up?" to "can I have one?" ... in five minutes flat.

What I learned was: for most people, the bar for AI isn't perfection. It’s “is this helping me get what I need?” The moment someone feels like the conversation is ACTUALLY helping them, the question of whether they're talking to AI completely disappears.
