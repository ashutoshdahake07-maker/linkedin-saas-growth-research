AI is disintermidating status in the buying journey.

When websites first came out they were built with basic HTML & CSS.

They were uggggly.
But very fast & accessible.

Then - HTML5, WebGL, CSS3 (and even Flash which the IPhone killed back in the early 2010’s) and other technologies enabled slick websites.

Website design & interactivity became a symbol of status. It would communicate whether this is a luxury (Apple’s website) or commodity product (Ryanair’s website).

Websites stopped being about functional information - and became about an experience…a extension of brand.

But what use is investing in slick design when an AI agent running research or a comparison for a buyer who doesn’t take design into consideration?

What’s the economic case for investing in design for what is 1/5th or 1/20th of the same number of visitors - which will continue to dwindle further.

Interestingly visitors from answer engines often arrive later in the decision process. The visit is a result of less exploratory work and more confirmation work.

An advantage for buyers (like the shift to inbound was with the advent of search & social & online reviews) but less so for sellers.

The inbound era removed the access asymmetry (buyers could now find information). The AI era is removing the processing asymmetry (buyers no longer have to evaluate it themselves).

In many ways, science (data & information) is winning against human bias.
