End of last year, bots were 44% of all traffic. Now it's 57.5% according to Cloudflare (20% of the web traffic goes through their servers).

So your first instinct may be: so what? 
It doesn't change anything because AI is just modelling what humans are interested in. So just keep writing for humans, right?

But then if that was the case, then there wouldn't have been an SEO role created 20 years ago to optimise websites for search. 
You'd just write for humans...

The key thing marketers now need to get a grasp on is:
- How LLMs fundmentally work
- What's that mean in terms of their role in search/research
- How to optimise for them
- How to leverage them for performance

This point of view of SEO = AEO misses a greater consideration still. This isn't only web crawling - these are agentic deployments running research for ABM, curating target lists, competitive research etc'

The world is shifting quickly. Don't hold on to outdated mental models - shed what doesn't serve you.
