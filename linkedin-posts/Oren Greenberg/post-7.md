Google is testing new ad formats, which means their ad revenue will shift to this new AI-agentic world. Huge shift.

On first glance, you might think, 'so before we had ads on Google when we searched...and now we'll have the same ads chatting to Gemini the AI agent, so what?'

Not quite.

The ads are conversational. They are rich context specific responses to the user's query. Which, with personalisation + history can become fairly long tail.

Meaning that rather than just bid on a keyword and add some generic ad copy, you need your content to do some heavy lifting e.g.
- Who it’s for
- What problem it solves
- Why it’s different
- Proof points
- Trade-offs
- Pricing/use cases
- Who it’s not for

So now your focus is on optimising for being understood and recommended by AI instead...
This means understanding how AI discerns what is relevant. 
Before, it was keywords + ad copy & landing page copy / experience, user bounce rate & click-through rate.

User behaviour defined what a good ad means in essence.

The key shift is that humans still decide eventually. But AI mediates what you see, how options are framed, and which brands are considered relevant. Google’s AI becomes the interpreter between buyer intent and advertiser relevance.

If Gemini is generating explainers, advertisers need to control the content it learns from:
- Product feeds
- Website copy
- FAQs
- Comparison pages
- Reviews
- Pricing pages
- Structured data
- Case studies
- Help docs
- Third-party mentions (big factor for AI visibility ranking in LLMs, not clear how relevant will be for ads but probably not major I'd predict)

Ironically, product marketing functions will become more important for optimising search than search marketers have been for the past +20 years...

That means:
- Build content around real buyer questions
- Make positioning explicit and structured
- Feed Google clear product/service data
- Monitor whether AI explainers describe you accurately
- Measure quality of AI-assisted leads, not just CTR

Ads have always been hated due to poor relevance. Google ads have always been the best of the bad bunch because they only show you relevant ads with high intent. Now it's taken it to a whole new level of personalisation.

It's a major shift from traffic first to relevance & depth. Welcome to a new world where everyone wins (well almost, because I'm sure someone is losing out, but not sure who).
