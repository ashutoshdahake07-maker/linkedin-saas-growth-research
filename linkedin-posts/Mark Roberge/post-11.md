Cold calls or cold emails?

I was talking with my friend Jacco van der Kooij recently about this exact debate. And like most GTM decisions, the answer has less to do with the channel and everything to do with the context.

Cold calls carry a human element you simply can’t replicate — real conversation, real diagnosis. For individuals or tightly defined account lists, it’s one of the highest-leverage motions you can run.

Cold emails scale. They let you reach far more prospects with targeted personalization at a fraction of the effort. When your ICP is broad and your budget is tight, email becomes a powerful volume engine.

The Science of Scaling framework outlines that an outbound strategy has to map to the economics:
Who are you targeting?
What is their ACV?
How much time and money can you justify per prospect?

High-touch channels make sense when every deal meaningfully moves the model.
Low-touch channels win when efficiency is the constraint.

Less of a hypothetical debate, and more an ROI-based decision — made in the real context of your business.
