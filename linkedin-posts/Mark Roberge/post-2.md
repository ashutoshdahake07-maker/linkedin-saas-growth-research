
At this moment in the AI era, we are seeing:
- dramatically increased product development velocity.
- explosive demand from the market to buy and experiment with AI solutions.

As a result, many companies have developed broad product portfolios and achieved revenue growth levels in months that used to take years to realize. 

Yet two critical factors don't seem to be keeping pace:
1) The speed at which buyers successfully adopt these products.
2) The speed at which customers realize meaningful business value.

This got me thinking.

Is the resource allocation across our organizations optimized for this moment?

For example, if our resource allocation is...
• 50% Product & Engineering
• 30% Sales & Marketing
• 20% Customer Success and Forward-Deployed Engineering

... is that aligned with the customer adoption and value realization bottleneck standing in the way of an even faster-growing, more durable business model?
