One of the most common mistakes I see in early-stage founder selling is the “false positive.”

The buyer says all the right things:
“We love it.”
“This is a priority.”
“Call us in 3 months when you finish the MVP and we’ll move forward.”

Then 3 months later… they ghost the founder.

Instead, try this:

Tell the buyer you’re looking for a small group of design partners who will commit to trying the product when it’s ready. Ask them to sign a Letter of Intent (LOI), place a small deposit, and offer a meaningful first-year discount in exchange for their willingness to be a "guinea pig" and provide continual feedback.

At this stage, your goal isn’t profit maximization. It’s getting the product into the hands of committed early customers and learning whether they actually realize the value you intended.

If they say "no" to signing the LOI, the real objections will now come out. Learning accelerates

This approach:

1. Eliminates false positives
2. Surfaces real objections early
3. Reduces friction around elements you don’t need to optimize yet (e.g., pricing)
4. Naturally attracts early adopters—who make the best design partners

An LOI isn’t about paperwork.

It’s about separating signal from noise.
