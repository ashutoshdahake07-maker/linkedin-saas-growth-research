AI gives us the ability to analyze problems through an almost infinite set of considerations.

Take ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) formulation.

As a go-to-market ecosystem, we’ve historically defined ICPs using a narrow set of firmographic attributes — revenue, employee count, industry, geography. Not because those are perfect predictors… but because that’s what humans could reasonably process.

But from a first-principles perspective, that’s not what an ICP is. An ICP should identify the accounts:
• Your product was actually built for
• That will realize the most value
• In turn, generate the highest LTV for your business
• And can be acquired and served at a cost where your unit economics work

AI allows us to optimize our ICP definition at a far more sophisticated level.
Instead of relying on static firmographics, we can incorporate qualitative insights, hidden patterns, and nuanced signals — and actually optimize how we deploy go-to-market resources.

If you have 50 hours of sales capacity this month to pursue new accounts, the question becomes:

Are you deploying that time against the best possible new accounts?

Here’s a simple process to start building an AI-powered ICP model using your team’s collective intuition:
1 - Input your current firmographic ICP to train the model on the existing baseline
2 - Have 3 AEs and 3 CSMs evaluate 10 prospective accounts
3 - Ask them to rank the accounts from best to worst
4 - Capture an unstructured transcript of them describing why they ranked the accounts accordingly
5 - Repeat the exercise with the same team against 10 existing customers
6- Feed all of this qualitative data into an AI model
7- Let it identify patterns beyond revenue, industry, and geography

Let AI do what it does best: see the patterns beyond human capability.
