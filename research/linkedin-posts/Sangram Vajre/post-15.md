
had a call last week with a 3x CMO who went fractional.

she's been doing this for 2 years. great clients. solid work.

but she said something that stuck with me:

"i feel like just another consultant competing on price."

so i asked her a few questions:

how do you charge? "hourly or day rate."

how do you position yourself? "i tell them i can do anything they need."

do you have a framework? "not really... i just figure it out each time."

and there it was.

she wasn't building a fractional business.

she was freelancing with a fancier title.

so i put together this graphic to show her the difference.

freelancer:
→ hourly rates, competing on price
→ "i can do anything"
→ tasks and execution
→ treated like a vendor
→ income ceiling: $50-80K/year

fractional:
→ fixed-fee retainers, attracts through expertise
→ "i solve this one problem"
→ strategy and outcomes
→ trusted advisor at the table
→ income potential: $250K+/year

same 20 years of experience.

completely different business.

the gap isn't skill. it's positioning, packaging, and pricing.

she looked at it and said: "why didn't anyone tell me this earlier?"

that's why i'm sharing it with you.

which side are you on right now?
