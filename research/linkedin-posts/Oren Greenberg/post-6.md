You know how the subscription tiers for ChatGPT & Claude are heavily subsidised, right?

People reach this conclusion by comparing the cost of running the same level of activity (measured in tokens) to the API cost.

But...the API costs are fabricated.

Like all pricing is.

These companies aren't pricing by cost plus. They are pricing by value.

The majority of the costs are people + infrastructure + compute for future growth. 
Power users are a minority, subsidised by 'light' users who pay $20 and barely use it.

Makes it tempting to think these companies are sitting on a hidden profit machine. Just stop spending and harvest.
Looking at open source models for comparison, DeepSeek cited a 545% margin on inference alone.

But can the AI jagguernuts pull the budget? Nope.
Because of competitive forces. 

There are many advantages to leveraging economies of scale. Survival being one of them.

If these companies raised the subscription fee to match the API costs, they wouldn't make a fortune. They'd churn customers at a rate faster than people were escaping the Titanic. The majority would migrate to open-source models.

Competitive forces (frontier & open-source) releasing new models further push prices down. The only option for winning here is to be the best (smartest LLM).

Every query individually is profitable. But the companies aren't. Because the bill for the next model resets to zero every new release. It's a treadmill they can't step off of...
And apparently, one we can't either.
