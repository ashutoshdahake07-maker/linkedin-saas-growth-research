I've got a publicly listed client who wasn't sure about their AEO strategy and commissioned an audit...

AI search visibility has become even more important now with Google (finally) changing its search box to a Gemini-powered AI-first experience (affecting roughly 2.5bn people).

Expect AI search to be the number 1 priority for marketers in the near future, until the dust settles and it becomes commonplace.
I say that...but even after 20 years, I see Google Ads accounts in dire straits. Which is surprising, since G Ads had more official support than SEO ever had + more transparency over how it worked under the hood.

Why such poor performance you ask?
As digital marketing evolved, roles also developed, resulting in SEO/PPC progressively migrating to developing countries. That isn't to say the quality of work in these countries as a whole is poorer. But rather, there's less predictability in expected quality.

I still see UK/EU/US managed accounts, and they are in an abysmal state - this usually happens when a full-service agency has a clueless junior manning the post. If you're going down this route, go with a specialist.

Back to AEO: It's worth noting there's no shortage of players in the market that provide visibility on AEO rankings.
So why did I build my own? (which took less than a day in Claude code using Openrouter + dataforseo).

To set a benchmark I'm confident in for my client.
But why? What's wrong with the +85 players in the space.

The issue is that many are scraping the results directly from the free version of ChatGPT / Perplexity et al. And the ones scraping the UI with pro accounts are suffering from bias due to personalisation. That means historical searches are biasing future searches - and if you're doing that in volume, you're getting very skewed results.

It's also worth noting that a B2C audience (on free tiers) is markedly different from a B2B audience (the majority will be on a paid tier with an LLM).

Why do they scrape the UI you ask?
Because it's cheaper. When you're running huge volumes of prompts (which you must do to sift through what is noise to identify a meaningful pattern in probabilistic stochastic models, which LLMs are).

It's also worth noting that using an API directly is superior to using tools like Exa / Tavily / Parallel because they don't include an option to send geo-location. Critical for assessing visibility for a multinational brand. Those tools are ace for building specific target accounts lists for ABM but not for this use-case.

I'm not advocating for you building your own in-house either. Building a prototype to pull some queries is one thing. Maintaining that overtime is far more complex.
