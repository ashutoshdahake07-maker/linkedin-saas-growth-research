Everyone is selling you AI-this or AI-that.

From Antrhopic (makers of Claude) in their 'Mapping the Mind of a Large Language Model' research paper:
“We mostly treat AI models as a black box: something goes in and a response comes out, and it’s not clear why the model gave that particular response instead of another.”

In the simplest terms: AI is unpredictable.

So does that mean you shouldn't use it?
Yes & no.

No:
- Don't use it for areas that are critical and require a predictable & reliable result (data scientists call this deterministic) e.g.
1. Reporting numbers to the board. Pipeline figures, CAC, LTV, attribution — these need to tie back to the source system exactly

2. Sending anything customer-facing without a human in the loop

3. Brand voice and positioning decisions. AI can inform, but it shouldn't decide

Yes:
- Use it for areas where mistakes are less costly (examples where I deploy for my own projects or clients):
1. Data-heavy analysis that requires running Python scripts (which Claude/ChatGPT do a great job of generating) where I am confident I can evaluate the accuracy of the output

2. First drafts of content, such as LinkedIn posts, sales emails, landing page copy, ad variations etc'

3. Pre-call research and prospect synthesis, running online research to gather info

AI is still great at synthesising, summarising, and extracting text.

Use AI where being roughly right beats being slowly right. Avoid it where being exactly right is the whole point. A brilliant intern at some things, not a calculator. Treat it like one.
