biggest mistake I see founders make: 

trying to abdicate the responsibility of sales.

most founders don't come from a sales background...

and sales is tough at first. 

but that's why you should lean into it...as much as possible.

nothing moves the needle in a business like sales, and founders have a superpower when it comes to sales. 

Something about the founder getting on a call instantly increases close rate, confident in your buyers, and the effectivenss of (what early on is surely) an imperfect sales process.

delegate support, delegate development, delegate product design. but don't delegate sales. IT should be the last thing you take off your plate.
