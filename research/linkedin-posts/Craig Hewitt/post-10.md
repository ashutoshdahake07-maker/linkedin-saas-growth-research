Everyone's talking about AI agents. 

But let me cut through the noise.

An AI agent is simply an AI that does stuff. Not just answers questions...actually completes tasks in the real world.

Booking travel. 
Creating client reports. 
Processing PRs. 
Replying to emails. 
Managing logistics.

We've moved from "hey AI, what do you think about this?" to "hey AI, go do this."

-> But here's where it gets interesting for founders. <-

Anthropic just released Agent Teams in Claude Code: multiple AI agents that actually collaborate. 

They share context, check in with each other, and divide work like a real team should.

Think of it like this:

You're planning a retreat with 4 families. One parent handles the Airbnb. Another researches hikes. Someone else figures out restaurants. They talk to each other (unless your friends is disfunctional....which, happens) the hiking person coordinates with the restaurant person on locations, the Airbnb person and restaurant person align on dietary restrictions.

Agent Teams works exactly like that. 
A team lead divvies up tasks. Teammates own their areas. They communicate through shared context and direct messages.

Why this matters as you're running a company

This isn't about replacing your team. It's about giving yourself (the CEO) capabilities you couldn't afford or justify hiring for.

I had Claude spin up an agent team to stress-test a client's Q1 growth strategy: 

+ one agent as skeptical CFO poking holes in financials
+ one as competitor analyst
+ one as operations lead flagging execution risks. 

They debated each other and synthesized a brutal-but-constructive memo with prioritized action items.

That analysis would have taken a consulting team a week. 

It took 20 minutes.

Ok, now the caveats:

- It's experimental. Expect some friction.
- It burns through tokens fast. Budget accordingly.
- Don't use it for simple tasks. You don't need 4 agents to write an email.
- Best for work that can be split up but needs shared context.

The CEOs who learn to orchestrate AI agent teams will operate at a completely different level than those still copy-pasting into ChatGPT.

Here's the thing: the interface for how we work with AI is changing faster than most founders realize. 

Single prompts became conversations. 

Conversations became agents. 

Agents are now becoming teams.

The question isn't whether this will change how you run your company. 

It's whether you'll figure it out before your competitors do.
