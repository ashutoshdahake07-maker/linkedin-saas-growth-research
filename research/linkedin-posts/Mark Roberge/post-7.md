One of the most common failure points I see in startup scaling isn't running out of money or losing to a competitor.

It's scaling before the business is actually ready — and not having a rigorous way to know the difference.

I recently joined Brian Kenny on Harvard Business School's Cold Call podcast to discuss an HBS case I wrote on InsightSquared, a software startup that found itself at exactly this inflection point. They had customers, revenue, and a Series A. And they were asking the right question: are we actually ready to scale?

A few things I think are worth reflecting on:

Product market fit is not a revenue milestone. It's whether the majority of customers you acquire are seeing the value you promised them. Signing a contract has very little to do with that.

The go-to-market system is not a static optimization. The hiring profile, comp plan, demand gen strategy, and sales methodology all need to be designed for your specific context — who you're selling to, what stage you're at, how complex the product is — and continually re-evaluated.

The north star in the early days shouldn't be a revenue target. It should be the leading indicator of retention, what you can observe in the first weeks of a customer relationship that predicts whether they'll stay.
