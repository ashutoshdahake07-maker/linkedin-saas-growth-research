This week I had the opportunity to join the Topline podcast with Asad Zaman, AJ Bruno and Sam Jacobs — we covered a lot of ground, but three things stuck out in this conversation:

1) The AI Bubble: Why the index fund of the last two years of AI investments is likely doomed.

I have high conviction that the company-level failure rate in this AI cohort will be historically high. Not because AI is hype, but because too many “AI-native” products are workflow streamlining dressed up as reinvention. If an incumbent can copy it in 6–12 months with existing distribution, it’s not a moat.

2) Fixing Your ICP: Optimizing for CAC or inbound volume without ICP fundamentals is a recipe for disaster. 

Founders still define ICP by where inbound is highest, CAC is lowest, or close rates look best. That’s backwards. Even HubSpot only treated ~30% of inbound as true ICP. The right anchor is retention and expansion. Early on, define a leading indicator of retention: p% of customers do event e every t time. Build around that.

3) The 80% Rule: How AI moves reps from 25% selling time to 80%, and what that means for SDR, AE, and CS.

Most reps spend 25–30% of their week actually selling. The rest is admin and coordination. If AI drives selling time to 75–80%, the math of the org changes. The SDR → AE → AM → CS assembly line starts to collapse. Fewer handoffs. More ownership. Higher quality.
