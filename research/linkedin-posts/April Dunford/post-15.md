In tech companies, I often find different members of the exec team have very different opinions about basic components of positioning. 

The leaders of Sales and Product often disagree on who the company competes with. The founder often disagrees with Product or Marketing about the unique value the product delivers for customers. Customer success will often disagree with the product team on what our true product strengths are. 

The interesting thing is that nobody is wrong, and everyone is correct. Each group sees the customer in different situations and at different points in the purchase journey. Often, groups are talking to different types of customers in very different contexts. Things look a certain way in certain situations. 

It's a bit like that old parable where a group of people with blindfolds on touch a different part of an elephant. The person touching the trunk says, "It's a snake!" The person touching the tail says, "It's a rope!" and the person on the leg says, "It's a tree!" We need to bring the parts together to understand the elephant.

If we really want to understand what we've got, we need input from everyone. I think great positioning comes from bringing marketing, product, sales, customer success, and the founder together. We need everyone's input, and each group needs to understand what the other groups see.
